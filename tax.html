📊 Indian Stock Tax Calculator & Education

💡 Comprehensive Tax Education for Indian Investors (2024-25)

📈 1. Short Term Capital Gains (STCG)

Gains from sale of listed equity shares or equity mutual funds held for less than 12 months. Tax rate is 15% (Section 111A) when Securities Transaction Tax (STT) is paid. Losses can be set off against other capital gains and carried forward for up to 8 years.

📉 2. Long Term Capital Gains (LTCG)

Gains from listed equity shares/mutual funds held for more than 12 months. Gains up to ₹1 lakh annually are tax-free; above that taxed at 10% (Section 112A) without indexation benefit. Losses can be set off and carried forward for 8 years.

🔄 3. Intraday Trading (Speculative Business Income)

Buying and selling shares on the same day. Considered speculative income under Section 43(5), taxed as per normal slab rates (up to 30%). Business expenses like brokerage, internet, advisory can be claimed.

📊 4. Futures & Options (F&O Trading)

Classified as non-speculative business income. Taxed as per individual slab rates. Allowed to deduct business expenses. Audit is required if turnover exceeds ₹10 crores or profit is less than 6% of turnover.

💵 5. Dividends

Dividend income from stocks and mutual funds is taxed as per slab rates. If annual dividend exceeds ₹5,000, companies deduct 10% TDS. No exemption available from FY 2020-21 onwards.

💸 6. Other Income

Includes interest from savings, fixed deposits, rental income, and side business income. Taxed under respective heads at normal slab rates. Must be aggregated for final tax calculation.

⚙️ Key Tax Planning Tips:

  • Losses must be reported in ITR to carry forward and set off.
  • Business income (F&O/Intraday) should consider proper expense tracking.
  • Investments in ELSS, PPF, NPS qualify for deductions under Section 80C and 80CCD.
  • Dividend stripping and bonus stripping rules apply to avoid artificial losses.
  • Tax audit and maintenance of books mandatory if trading turnover exceeds prescribed limits.
